Temperature and Humidity Control in an
Evaporative Cooling AC System

Project Introduction

This project developed a combined evaporative cooling and desiccant wheel (EC/DW) system for residential temperature and humidity control, aiming for substantial energy and emissions savings compared to conventional AC systems. The objectives were to create a mathematical and simulation model for house thermal and humidity dynamics, design a closed-loop MIMO (temperature & humidity) EC/DW system with ANN controller, and compare the system's energy use and sustainability performance with a standard vapor-compression AC. The project demonstrates how modern controls and hybrid HVAC technology can make sustainable cooling feasible even in humid climates.

Methodology

  • Dynamic Modeling: Differential equations and Simulink models for temperature and humidity of residential spaces.
  • Controller Design: PID and artificial neural network (ANN) approaches for setpoint tracking and disturbance rejection.
  • Performance Evaluation: Simulation of open- and closed-loop responses for temperature and humidity, plus energy and emissions analysis.

Key Results

  • Closed-loop EC/DW system achieved <3% steady-state error for temperature and humidity setpoints.
  • Energy savings and emissions reduction up to 80% compared to vapor-compression AC.
  • Demonstrated feasibility of advanced control (ANN) for residential HVAC systems.
