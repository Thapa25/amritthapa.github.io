Closed Circuit Subsonic Wind Tunnel

Design & Analysis of a Low-Speed Wind Tunnel with Axial Fan

Project Overview

For my undergraduate final year project, I undertook the complete design and analysis of a closed-circuit low-speed subsonic wind tunnel, including the selection and design of an axial fan to power the flow. The goal was to develop a reliable methodology, starting from literature review to analytical calculations, and then validating results with computational simulations.

Abstract

This research presents a comprehensive methodology for designing a subsonic closed-circuit wind tunnel, including the analytical and simulation-based approach to both the tunnel channel and the powering axial fan. The design targeted a test section of 2.6 m width and 1.84 m height with a maximum velocity of 100 m/s. Key design and loss parameters were calculated and then validated through CFD simulations of the entire tunnel and the fan under different operating conditions. The wind tunnel achieved high flow uniformity (within 1% at mid-plane and 3% overall) and low turbulence intensity (<0.17%). The axial fan, designed using isolated airfoil theory and free vortex methodology, achieved 89% analytical efficiency and 80.85% simulated efficiency at the design point.
