Closed Circuit Subsonic Wind Tunnel

Design & Analysis of a Low-Speed Wind Tunnel with Axial Fan

Project Overview

This project presents the complete design and analysis of a closed-circuit subsonic wind tunnel, including an axial fan, using analytical methods and CFD simulation. I developed the methodology, conducted full-channel simulations, and validated design parameters for velocity, flow uniformity, and turbulence intensity in the test section.

Abstract

The design targeted a test section of 2.6 m width and 1.84 m height with a maximum velocity of 100 m/s. Geometry and loss parameters were determined analytically, then validated by CFD for design and off-design conditions. The wind tunnel achieved flow uniformity within 1% in mid plane, within 3% overall, and turbulence intensity under 0.17%. A single-stage axial fan was designed and achieved 89% analytical efficiency and 80.85% CFD efficiency at 853 Pa pressure rise.
