Evaporative Cooling Condenser
for Air-Conditioning

Jump Into STEM Challenge | University of Texas at Tyler

Team: Amrit Thapa, Kristinoba Olotu, Oluwanisola Makinde, Rashish Adhikari

Overview

This project proposes an evaporatively cooled condenser retrofit for residential air-conditioning in hot-dry climates. By adding evaporative media, the system achieves a 24% reduction in compressor work and CO₂ emissions, saving billions of lbs of CO₂ annually.

Background & Problem

Rising AC usage in low-income areas drives up energy costs and emissions. Existing systems force households to choose between comfort and essentials.

  • Albuquerque: 43% low-income, AC = 10%+ energy use.
  • CO₂ emissions: 846.6 lbs/MWh in target zones.

Solution & Design

  • Add evaporative media to increase heat rejection.
  • Reduce compressor work by up to 24% at design point.
  • Use standard refrigerant (R134a) for safety.
  • Annual CO₂ savings: 4.45 billion lbs in region.

Key Results

  • Compressor work ↓: 24% avg (Albuquerque), 7% (Las Vegas)
  • CO₂ ↓: 4.45 × 10⁹ lbs/year regionally; 440 lbs/home/year
  • Energy savings: 5.26 × 10⁹ kWh/year

Example: 3-ton AC at Albuquerque → 41% work saved → ~$600/home (PV over 15 yrs, 10% discount).

Technology-to-Market & Feasibility

  • PV savings up to $600/home (15 yrs, 10% discount).
  • Retrofit is plug-and-play for existing units.
  • Market via manufacturer partnerships, DoE/ARPA-E grants, patent filings.
  • Barriers (cost, training) mitigated by education & subsidies.
