Evaporative Cooling Condenser
for Air-Conditioning

Jump Into STEM Challenge | University of Texas at Tyler

Team: Amrit Thapa, Kristinoba Olotu, Oluwanisola Makinde, Rashish Adhikari

Overview

This project proposes an evaporatively cooled condenser retrofit for residential air conditioning units in mixed/hot, dry climates (Albuquerque, NM & Las Vegas, NV). By adding evaporative media to the condenser, the system achieves an average 24% reduction in compressor work and CO₂ emissions, directly benefiting energy-burdened, low-income communities. The concept is technically and economically viable, offering significant savings and a reduction of 4.5 billion lbs CO₂ emissions annually in the target region.

Background & Problem

AC usage is rising rapidly in hotter, low-income areas-driving energy costs and emissions higher, particularly for renters and disadvantaged groups. Existing AC systems in these regions are energy-intensive, causing households to choose between comfort and essentials.

  • Albuquerque: 43% of households are low-income, 10%+ of energy is for AC.
  • CO₂ emissions: AC-related emissions are 846.6 lbs/MWh in these zones.

There is a pressing need for affordable retrofits that cut energy use, lower utility bills, and reduce carbon footprint in these communities.

Solution & Design

  • Add evaporative media to the AC condenser, increasing heat rejection and reducing refrigerant temperature.
  • Compressor work is reduced by up to 24% at design conditions.
  • Retains use of common refrigerant (R134a) for environmental safety.
  • Annual emissions reduction: 4.45 billion lbs CO₂ in the region.

Key Results

  • Compressor work reduction: Avg. 24% (Albuquerque), up to 7% (Las Vegas)
  • CO₂ reduction: 4.45 billion lbs/year (region), 440 lbs/home/year
  • Energy savings: 5.26 billion kWh/year in the mixed hot-dry climate zone
Example calculation: 3-ton AC, R134a refrigerant, 41% compressor work saved (Albuquerque), present value savings ≈ $600/home (15 years, 10% discount rate).

Technology-to-Market & Feasibility

  • Present value of 15-year savings: up to $600 per home (10% discount rate).
  • Retrofit feasible for existing AC units; easily scalable for low-income homes.
  • Market plan: manufacturer partnerships, DoE validation, ARPA-E funding, and patenting.
  • Barriers: initial investment, awareness, installation training—addressed by education & grants.
